Cirque du Huayra is a pack by Zaia. It is a “rolling release” pack, which means that it receives new content on an ongoing basis.

It is specifically a pack of restepped charts only (simfiles first made by other people that I later stepped and released myself).
It is named after the Pagani Huayra, and is themed after the Japanese manga Made in Abyss.

 

 

 

Structure

This pack differs from the others in that it has no fixed structure other than that every simfile has at least one chart, and that all charts are classified as stream charts. Some simfiles may have lowers, and difficulty levels span a broad range.

The main commonality of this pack is that every simfile in it had previously been stepped before; not necessarily just by someone else, but I could have restepped my own older charts and placed them here too.

Development

 

The project became more serious when the Stamina Showcase 2 stream simfile competition was announced in April 2018. I took a two-month stepping break after the Jan 03 releases of Cirque du Enzo, Rebuild of Sharpnel and Cirque du Jackhummer, and then came back for two weeks to start work on Saitama's Ultimate Weapon, and then went on break again. I needed to do some stepping to get myself back into stepping mode properly. I elected to step some Cranked Pastry songs to undergo this training, since it was quite early in Stamina Showcase 2 that I decided I would be submitted a dark psytrance song (KOKOBLOKO - Life burst (Liquid E.T. Remix)).

After Stamina Showcase 2 ended, the project continued, and the other simfiles in the pack filtered into existence in ones and twos every now and then. 

Artistic Influences

The style that dominates this pack was established by the Cirque du Enzo simfile for Longinus, which at the time was my best chart. I wanted to take these new leveled-up skills and apply them to other songs; particularly dark psytrance songs, which were the most challenging genre of music to step. Longinus was relatively “easy” to step because it had a dynamic foreground melody and plenty of layers to work with - contrasted with dark psytrance which had no distinct foreground layer and consisted mainly of monotonous rolling bass interspersed with sound effects that simulated a melody. It would be a real test of skills and mettle.

All six of the Cranked Pastry resteps were done over the course of ten days. Something very notable to me was the significant increase in “stepping stamina” that I now had. I was reminded of how challenging and gruelling each song had been, and how physically enervated I felt after finishing just a single chart. But now, I stepped five in a row and felt “nothing”.

One curious rhetoric choice was the reduced use of UXU and DXD patterns (UU/DDs or “vertical steppiness” in the modern vernacular). This was in the early stages of pattern minmaxing, which was the purposeful discrimination between pattern use to appeal to certain playstyles. UU/DDs were my enemy #1 during this period and was making an active effort to use them as little as possible. Doing this helped place an artificial restriction on my patterning and forced me to optimize what I was using, and it resulted in better clarification of patterning, particularly of UU/DD use (they would have a greater impact whenever they were used).

Reception and Retrospect

Cirque du Huayra released quietly and to a muted reception, and it wasn't marketed as a major pack in my simfilography. Its charts are mainly used when the songs are being featured in ECS events - the combination of familiar song with updated steps is a lucrative combination for use in the metagame. Generally, I haven't seen this pack see regular play outside of events.

At this stage of my career (late-2018 onwards), I wasn't really doing resteps anymore, like I had been in the first few years, and was more focused on original content. That trend has continued, and its resulted in the pack gaining zero new charts in years. I would very much like to continue adding content to the pack, but this isn't the kind of pack I make a point of actively working on; its more a pack in the old style of what a pack used to be - a collection of singles that were bundled together after a stepartist had amassed enough of them.

There's actually been enough time now that I could restep the entire pack again and the charts would be noticeably different.

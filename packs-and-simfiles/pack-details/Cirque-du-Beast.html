Cirque du Beast is a pack by Zaia, under the Serenade name. 

It is the first in what would become the Cirque series, spawning a multitude of spinoff pack lines bearing the name.

 

 

Structure

Cirque du Beast was to be my first “true” pack, after the long period of incubation and growth that followed S21 Works. The songs featured were carefully selected so the songlist could represent artists and music types that were meta at the time:

  • 30MIN HARDER and Cyber Inductance by DJ Sharpnel were used, as longer form stamina was in vogue with the ongoing popularity of Archi's Disbalance 3
  • Air War had a more video-gamey sound and fit a chart type that I myself needed (long burst 240 training)
  • Ascension to Heaven, Atmosphere, Belladonna, Cynthia and Protosphere were decently-paced songs with gentler sound design, starting my predilection for high speed music made with classical instruments
  • At the Speed of Light, by Dimrain47, reflected the high popularity of songs like Infernoplex and other Newgrounds type songs by the artist. Including Hyperion Overdrive was an attempt to add a new potential artist to this genre
  • Atmosphere represented something slower and longer but with a sound that wasn't distinctly trance. It also bore some pedigree, coming from the Mungyodance series (at the time)
  • I'm Falling Away (8-Bit Mix) by m1dlet was included as a nod to the SPEEEDCOOOORE pack, which was also metagame content in early 2012 (Happy Feet was also stepping a lot of speedcore around this time, which boosted the genre's perceived presence)
  • Isolation, by Nighthawk22, had decent popularity through Mad Matt's charts to the same song (Causality Violation) and to Corrosion (Subluminal)
  • Joker was by Tatsh, the composer of popular Bemani song Xepher, and featured Hatsune Miku, the most famous Vocaloid
  • Mr Pokeahole and Metea11 represented the growing presence of Guitar Hero customs in the ITG metagame that appeared with Tachyon Gamma and Tachyon Delta
  • Nobody Said It Was Easy had a similar sound design to Vinyl
  • Nous was a fairly standard jcore type song from a safe rhythm game-friendly music producer
  • Superluminal was my breakout simfile, so it felt appropriate to feature this in the pack with lower difficulties to improve accessibility. Tranzport was a song with a similar simfile background (both from keyboard packs) and song structure

Dropbear Awareness Pack is a mixed stream/speed/casual simfile pack intended to be an Australian community simfile pack made by active members of the Australian ITG community.

It was primarily organized through the Revolution-Street forums (a popular Sydney-based forum for Australian DDR and ITG enthusiasts that was the de-facto national community hub, that is now defunct and was not archived)

 

 

Structure

Dropbear Awareness Pack is structured like a typical ITG customs pack. However, the chart structure is inconsistent- some songs contain full Novice-Expert difficulties, whilst some only contain Expert. The pack has a mixed genre of music types, given its multi-author contributions.

It was my job as the pack director to try to keep the pack focused in direction and scope, but as I was inexperienced, a lot of the pack's technical shortcomings went unaddressed and, in retrospect, I ended up micromanaging more of the pack than I should have.

Development

The pack's development was managed exclusively on the Revolution-Street forums during the latter half of 2011. Our community, though relatively small, was tight-knit and we made efforts to travel interstate to meet up and hang out. We had a fair number of players, but didn't really have a pack that we could call ours, so I proposed a project to help make that happen, since I knew we had a number of stepartists of varying degrees of prestige (most notably Jayce, who was a notable figure in the international community with his Gay4Gimmix series and low-tech projects doing well).

It was an open-invite pack, but Jayce I specifically reached out to, since he rarely posted on Rev-St and wouldn't have known about the project, and he graciously submitted one simfile. I carried most of the pack, since even during this time, I was a prolific stepartist and stepped a lot of songs. I also added difficulties to single charts to help fill the pack out more.

I can't recall too much else about the production otherwise, especially as the records of its development are now all gone. I recall wanting the pack to have good music/chart/difficulty variety to appeal to a broad range of players, but ended up predominantly being stream-focused.

It was never intended to be internationally released, and was only distributed on Rev-St itself.

Artistic Influences

A “Dropbear” is a fictitious sub-species of Koala that is carnivorous (real Koalas are herbivores) and is said to drop out of tree branches onto unsuspecting humans and attack them. It is a fairly well-known mythological creature in Australian folklore and has been referenced a number of times in popular media. 

The general pack structure was inspired by contemporary variety-style custom simfile packs of the time, which had no strict pack type or format, and which were often a stepartist's previously-released singles collated into a single folder for easy distribution.

Graphics

The pack has two banners; the pack banner and an individual simfiles fallback banner. I designed the pack banner, and JEDI designed the simfiles banner

There are a number of “stolen” graphics in this pack, which belonged to simfiles in other packs that I simply took for my own charts. These included: Aggressor (I believe it was taken from Cloud's version of the chart), Be Princess (Fabulously Fresh Flock of Fantastic Files F5), Cross Time (Dark Chancellors Pack), Gigadelic (Reach Originals), Hypnosis (I couldn't find a source for this one, but the stepartist name seems to be somebody by the alias “Invader 300”. Original chart the image is from is dated around August 2004).

Trivia

  • Despite the pack officially being produced under the ArkAngel name, the simfile folders themselves are attributed with either the Chino name or the Serenade name. The ArkAngel name only appears as the cdtitle graphic.
  • Chart metadata is horrendously inconsistent and incomplete. There are numerous fields of metadata from the previous stepartists whose skeletons I'd used that I had simply not removed. Some charts say "Copied From" and others say "Blank" in their description fields. Some have paths to graphics files that do not exist.
  • There are also numerous instances of incorrect titles and artist attribution:
    • Cyber Inductance was incorrectly titled as Cyber Induction, and the artist, DJ Sharpnel, was incorrectly attributed as Sharpnelsound, the label DJ Sharpnel was under.
    • WE LUV LAMA is also incorrectly attributed with the Sharpnelsound artist name.
    • Super Luminal was incorrectly titled as Superluminal, and the artist as the label S2TB Recording, instead of the artist kors k
  • The Superluminal chart in this pack is nearly identical to the one that was eventually placed into Tachyon Beta a year later. The S21 Works version contains a number of 16th bracket stomps and the long bursts of 24ths near the end of the chart were patterned differently.
  • A number of songs in this pack would later be restepped and re-released as future Zaia content. As of 2022, these include:
    • Snow Storm (Nothing is Sound, 2010)
    • Red Lucifer Rising (Ver-0) (Nothing is Sound, 2010)
    • WE LUV LAMA (Cirque du Veyron, 2012 and Rebuild of Sharpnel, 2018)
    • Atmos Break (Cirque du Lykan, 2014)
    • Destiny -Eclipse Mix- (Cirque du Zeppelin, 2014)
    • Cyber Inductance (Cirque du Beast, 2012 and Rebuild of Sharpnel, 2018)
    • Super Luminal (New Number Pack, TBA), with title and artist correctly attributed
  • This pack began the long-running tradition of referencing Neon Genesis Evangelion as part of the theme for a pack. Other packs to reference Evangelion include:
    • Cirque du Beast (2012)
    • Rebuild of Sharpnel (2018)
